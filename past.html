Dead Roads, Co Creator

Dead Roads is a play created by Neema Bickersteth, Darwin Lyons, Clare Preuss, and Zoe Sweet. We won the Ontario Arts Council Phase 1 & 2 development grants and three creator’s reserve grants. A workshop of this show was part of the 2016 RISER Project and was then developed with The National Arts Center’s October Oasis and Factory Theatre’s Residency Program. Check back for full production information

"With moments of stillness alternating with passionate speeches of desire, these Dead Roads are far from lifeless."

-Jon Kaplan, NOW​, Review of Dead Roads

Banff Citadel Training Program

I worked with Ravi Jain as the Associate Director of the Banff Citadel Training program. Ravi and his company, Why Not Theatre, curated the 2018/19 program, bringing an international focus to the teaching and training. The 2018/19 Citadel/Banff Centre Professional Theatre Program participants star in a reimagining of Shakespeare's The Tempest, directed by Josette Bushell-Mingo, former Artistic Director of Tyst Teater, Sweden’s National Deaf Theatre. The Citadel/Banff Centre Professional Theatre Program, part of The Citadel’s Academy, offers an advanced creative development program to enrich the performance skills of established theatre professionals. The 2019 program featured Deaf and Hearing actors.

RAE, Young People's Theatre

I was a Resident Artist Educator for Young People's Theatre for the 2017/18 and 2018/19 seasons. The Resident Artist Educator program at YPT furthers the theatre’s leadership in the field of arts education. Resident Artist Educators (RAEs) are selected each season to facilitate programming in the Education & Participation Department including Drama School, Educational and Community Programming. As both artists and educators, each participant creates, teaches and learns during their residency thereby embodying the company’s dual identity as a professional theatre and centre for arts education.

Centauri Arts

After attending as a camper, I have worked with Centauri Arts as a camp counsellor, a program director and as the inaugural drama instructor at Centauri Arts Academy. I have taught theatre, collective creation, and acting to ages 8-18, covering topics such as Shakespeare, classical theatre, modern theatre, voice and movement.

OAC Artist in Education

Paprika Festival, Artistic Producer

From 2015-2017 I worked for the Paprika Festival as the Artistic Producer. Paprika is an amazing organization, it is a year-round training program and festival for youth interested in all aspects of theatre. While there I was part of the creation of the first-ever strategic plan. I created a new program, The Director’s Lab, where young directors partnered with industry partners (Theatre Passe Muraille and Native Earth Performing Arts), as well as participated in master classes with directors, and had a workshop in the festival. I curated the programming for the festival, and oversaw all programming for The Regent Collective, The Creator’s Unit, The Playwrights’ Unit, The Writers’ Circle, The Director’s Lab, Productions, The Advisory Board, and The Intersection (a conference for young artists). I worked in all aspects of running the festival, from the minutia (scheduling, communications, day-to-day operations) to the overarching (grants, board relations, staffing, programming, curation). While there Paprika won the NOW Magazine Reader’s Choice award for best youth organization.
